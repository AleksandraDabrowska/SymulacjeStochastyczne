chi_squared <- function(N, n, p){
  E <- n*p
  wynik <- sum((N-E)^2/E)
  return(wynik)
}

N<-c()
licznik <- c(1:6)
for  (i in licznik){
  N[i] <-sum(wyniki==i)
}

probabilities <- c(1/6,1/6,1/6,1/6,1/6,1/6)

  1. What is the limit distribution of the median if n → ∞? Illustrate graphically. GUESS the theorem: if U1, . . . , Un are iid Uniform(0, 1) then bn(med(U1, . . . , Un) − a) → Normal(0, 1). Find appropriate a and bn. “Confirm” experimentally.

Medians have Beta distribution with expected value equal to 1/2 and varince 1/4(n+1). Using the CLT we have that the limit distribution is N(0,1). So we take a=1/2, bn=sqrt(4(n+1)).
